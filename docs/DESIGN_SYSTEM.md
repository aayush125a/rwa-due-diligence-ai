# DESIGN SYSTEM

> This document defines the visual language, UI principles, reusable
> components, and interaction guidelines for the AI-Powered Due Diligence
> Infrastructure for Real-World Asset Tokenization.
>
> The objective is to ensure every screen follows a consistent design language,
> creating a trustworthy experience suitable for compliance, financial
> institutions, businesses, and exchanges.

---

# 1. Design Philosophy

Our platform is not a consumer application.

It is professional financial infrastructure used to verify valuable Real-World
Assets before tokenization.

Every interface should communicate trust, clarity, precision, and transparency.

The user should immediately understand:

- what AI analyzed,
- what humans decided,
- why a decision was made,
- and what actions are available next.

The design should reduce cognitive load by presenting complex compliance
information in a structured and explainable manner.

---

## Core Design Principles

### Trust First

Every screen should inspire confidence through clean layouts, clear hierarchy,
and transparent information.

---

### Explainability Over Mystery

AI should never behave like a black box.

Every score, recommendation, and finding must be explainable.

---

### Humans Remain in Control

AI assists throughout the workflow, but important decisions are always confirmed
by human reviewers.

The interface should visually distinguish AI-generated content from
human-confirmed actions.

---

### Financial Software, Not Consumer Software

The interface should resemble enterprise compliance software rather than a
modern social media or productivity application.

Avoid unnecessary animations, decorative graphics, or playful UI elements.

---

### Consistency Everywhere

Colors, spacing, typography, score cards, badges, and status indicators should
remain consistent across every screen to reduce learning time and improve
usability.

---

# 2. Color System

The platform follows a restrained, professional color palette inspired by
enterprise financial software.

Colors are used intentionally rather than decoratively. Every color communicates
meaning and should remain consistent throughout the application.

---

## Primary Brand Color

**Primary Blue**

Purpose:

- Primary actions
- Navigation highlights
- AI-generated content
- Active components

Represents:

- Intelligence
- Trust
- Technology

---

## Success Green

Purpose:

- Approved assets
- Successful verification
- Human-confirmed decisions
- Positive status indicators

Represents:

- Verified
- Approved
- Trusted

---

## Warning Amber

Purpose:

- Medium risk
- Missing documents
- Pending review
- Attention required

Represents:

- Review Required
- Incomplete
- Caution

---

## Error Red

Purpose:

- High risk
- Failed verification
- Compliance issues
- Rejected assets

Represents:

- Critical Risk
- Rejection
- Fraud Indicators

---

## Neutral Gray

Purpose:

- Backgrounds
- Borders
- Disabled components
- Secondary information

Represents:

- Structure
- Balance
- Readability

---

## Background Colors

Use clean, bright backgrounds with subtle contrast between page sections and
content cards.

Avoid heavy gradients or overly saturated backgrounds.

The interface should always prioritize readability.

---

## Color Usage Rules

### AI-Generated Information

Use the Primary Blue color family with an "AI" badge to clearly indicate that
the content was generated by artificial intelligence.

---

### Human Decisions

Use Success Green or Error Red to represent confirmed decisions made by human
reviewers.

Every human action should display the reviewer's name and timestamp.

---

### Risk Indicators

Risk colors should always follow the same meaning:

- Green → Low Risk
- Amber → Medium Risk
- Red → High Risk

These colors should never be used decoratively elsewhere in the interface.

---

### Consistency Principle

Users should be able to recognize the meaning of every color instantly,
regardless of which screen they are viewing.
